About the Game

A creative way to remember digits

Euler's Golden Pie transforms abstract numbers into vivid images. Players encode digit sequences with peg cards, build memorable stories, and then use those stories to recall the original digits.

01

Encode

Convert digits into visual peg cards that represent numbers or number groups.

02

Build

Connect the peg cards into a story using actions, causes, tools, and consequences.

03

Recall

Decode the story back into the original digit sequence from memory.

The Core Loop

Digits → Peg Cards → Story → Recall

The power of the game comes from transforming numbers into meaningful associations. Instead of memorizing raw digits, players create a story that gives the sequence structure.

Rules

How to play

The goal is simple: remember the digit sequence better than your opponent. The challenge is how creatively and accurately you can encode it.

01

Select a Digit Card

Each player selects a digit card from one of the digit decks.

02

Encode the Digits

Players use peg cards to represent the digits in the selected sequence.

03

Build a Story

Players connect the selected peg cards into a memorable story.

04

Recall and Verify

Players exchange digit cards for verification and take turns reciting their sequences.

05

Win the Game

The player who correctly recalls the greatest number of digits in the original sequence wins.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Euler's Golden Pie?

Euler's Golden Pie is a memory-based strategy game where players turn digit sequences into stories using visual peg cards.

What is the goal of the game?

The goal is to correctly recall as many digits as possible from the original sequence.

How does the Encode Phase work?

Players represent digits using peg cards and arrange those cards in sequence.

How does the Build Phase work?

Players create a story using the selected peg cards so that the sequence becomes easier to remember.

How does the Recall Phase work?

Players use their story to reconstruct the original digit sequence from memory.

Why use stories to remember numbers?

Stories connect abstract digits to vivid images, actions, and associations, making the sequence easier to recall.
