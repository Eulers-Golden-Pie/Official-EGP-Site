About the Game

A creative way to remember digits

EGP transforms abstract numbers into visual associations. Players select digit cards, encode the sequence using peg cards, build a memorable story, and then recall the original digits.

Encode

Players convert digits into visual peg cards that represent numbers or number groups.

Build

The selected peg cards are connected into a story using actions, causes, and consequences.

Recall

Players use the story to reconstruct the original digit sequence from memory.

Rules

How to play

The goal is simple: remember the digit sequence better than your opponent. The challenge is how creatively you can encode it.

01

Select a Digit Card

Each player picks a digit card from one of the digit decks and sets it aside.

02

Encode the Digits

Players use peg cards to represent the digits in the selected sequence.

03

Build a Story

Players connect their peg cards into a memorable story that helps them recall the digits.

04

Recall and Verify

Players exchange digit cards for verification and take turns reciting their sequences.

05

Determine the Winner

The player who correctly recalls the greatest number of digits in the original sequence wins.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Euler's Golden Pie?

Euler's Golden Pie is a memory-based strategy game where players turn digit sequences into stories using visual peg cards.

How many players can play?

EGP is designed for competitive gameplay between players. Additional gameplay formats can be added depending on the rules being used.

How does the Encode Phase work?

During the Encode Phase, players represent digits using peg cards and organize those cards into a sequence.

How does the Build Phase work?

During the Build Phase, players create a story using the selected peg cards so that the sequence becomes easier to remember.

How is the winner decided?

The winner is the player who correctly recalls the greatest number of digits in the original sequence.

Why use stories to remember numbers?

Stories help connect abstract digits to vivid images, actions, and associations, making the sequence easier to recall.
